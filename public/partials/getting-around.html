    How to have a great Summit in five easy steps

    Participate

    The work of changing the world — the big, juicy Mozilla work — can happen only through active participation. Commit to pushing hard to go beyond what’s easy: embrace a new idea, ask a dumb question, lead a session, close your laptop. If a session does not move you, find another that does (it’s okay to shift). You alone get to decide what interests you, stretches you and ultimately helps you grow.

    Make Room

    Summit participants speak more than 114 languages. We don't all understand English fluently. Some of us are hard of hearing and visually impaired. When speaking, do it slowly and clearly. Check in with others as you go to make sure they understand.

    Some of us have physical limitations that make getting around difficult. Offer open structures with the idea of universal access, rather than waiting for special requests. Provide clear information on access.

    Make room (literally and metaphorically) for all before anyone has to ask. Seek to understand, then to be understood. Practice empathy. Assume positive intent.

    Practice Respect

    The Summit is a space where you can safely be your true self (why would you be anything else?) and a time to respect others’ true selves as well (even if it bothers you from time to time). Respect their space and freedom and uniqueness, as much as our own.

    Take care of yourself, body and mind. We only have three days together and they will be long. Mozilla needs you at your best. Rest, eat, drink water, laugh a lot.

    Take care of the space and each other. Pick up trash. Share your power cord.

    Be Helpful

    If you see a problem, try to help. If you can’t help, help find someone who can. Don’t quietly tolerate unacceptable behavior. Stand up for your fellow Mozillians. If someone’s words make you feel unsafe or uncomfortable — a comment, a joke, a slide — and can’t address it with them directly, consider involving an intermediary (like the Conductors, for example).

    If you’ve made someone feel unsafe or uncomfortable, be open to adjusting your presentation or public speech and apologize directly when needed.

    Have Fun

    The Summit is our opportunity to pause and celebrate who we are and all that we have accomplished. It’s a chance to meet other amazing humans, to connect, to dance, to build things, to break them too. It’s your moment to dig in and have fun.

    Let’s do it.

    Other things you may want to know

    Where to exchange money:

    The best exchange rate will be at a bank by withdrawing cash from using your ATM / debit card. There are also money exchange kiosks at the airports.

    The logos of the cards accepted are shown beside the machine. Most credit cards, including American Express and Diners Club, are accepted in numerous stores, boutiques, restaurants, car hire firms, etc.

    Currency:

    • Brussels - Euro
    • Toronto - Canadian Dollar (they may take US Dollar, but change will be in CAD)
    • Santa Clara - US Dollar

    Need Help:

    If you get lost, scared, confused, have a question, get hurt, need help finding someone, or need a power cord or adapter, we have an amazing group of Summit Site Hosts here to help. You can spot them because they are wearing blue hoodies that say "Summit Site Host".

    Alternatively, you can email: mozillabrussels@shworldwide.com OR mozillatoronto@shworldwide.com OR mozillasantaclara@shworldwide.com. There are lots of people here to help, know that.

    Accessibility Questions or Concerns:

    Head to the "Ask Us" desk or email mozillabrussels@shworldwide.com OR mozillatoronto@shworldwide.com OR mozillasantaclara@shworldwide.com.

    Wifi Connection Issues:

    Head to the "Ask Us" desk, we’ll try to help.

    Wifi Logins:

    • SC: SSID: Summit2013 PW: Mozilla#1
    • TO: SSID: Summit2013 PW: Mozilla
    • BR: SSID: Summit2013 PW: summit2013 (Square only)

    Electrical Outlets

    • Brussels - The standard voltage is 220V 50Hz. If you have equipment that runs on a lower voltage, you should not connect it to the Belgian power net unless you have the correct transformer (voltage adaptor). The Belgian power plug is the Western-European type, with two round pin-holes. Plugs from the UK, North and South America, and Asia will not fit into a Belgian electrical socket.
    • Toronto/Santa Clara - Voltages = 120V, 60 Hz and power plugs = Type A (NEMA 1–15 U.S. 2 pin) and Type B (NEMA 5–15 U.S. 3 pin)

    Official Languages

    • Brussels - Main language is French - also speak Dutch, German and English
    • Toronto - English and French
    • Santa Clara - English

    Parking

    • Brussels - hotels have self park - submit for reimbursement
    • Toronto - valet parking ($35/day) - validation at "Ask Us" desk, or parking across the street at City Hall ($26/day) and submit for reimbursement.
    • Santa Clara - Discounted self parking for $5 (validation available at the Ask Us desk)

    Taxis

    Ask the bell man at each hotel, they will flag one down for you. Do NOT accept a town car. If you only have a credit card, let the bellman know before they flag one for you.

    Tipping

    • All tips are taken care of within the venues.
    • Brussels - No tipping in restaurants, it is included already
    • Toronto/Santa Clara - customary to tip 15-20% on meals and services

    Nearest Mozilla space:

    • BRU: The nearest MozSpace is is Paris, France and also almost the same distance is to the office in London, UK
    • SC: The nearest MozSpace is in Mountain View, CA
    • TO: The nearest MozSpace is the one in town, Toronto

To accommodate enough time for travel, check-in, security, etc., we suggest you depart the hotel three hours before your flight and two hours before your train departure.

    • Brussels Airport Shuttle Hours: 6:30am - 8:00pm
    • Midi Train Station Shuttle Hours: 8:00am - 12:00pm

    There will be transportation experts in the lobby of your hotel to point you to the correct vehicle.

    Motorcoaches will await you at the lobby of your hotel to take you to Brussels Airport or Midi Train station.

    Traveling to Midi Train Station? Plan to depart your hotel at least 1.5 hours prior to your train departure time.

    Marriott Brussels: 8:15am - 1:15pm (last departure), departures every 30 minutes

    Radisson Blu Royal: 8:00am - 1:00pm (last departure), departures every 30 Minutes

    Royal Windsor Hotel: 8:15am - 12:00noon (last departure), departures every 30 Minutes
